Perth, the capital of Western Australia, sits at the heart of one of the world's most productive mining regions. The Pilbara iron ore belt, the Goldfields region, and the emerging lithium triangle collectively make Western Australia the world's largest exporter of iron ore and a dominant force in gold and lithium production. This extraordinary concentration of heavy industry creates an equally extraordinary demand for high-performance engineering plastics — materials that can withstand the punishing abrasion, impact, and chemical exposure inherent to mineral processing.

Why Perth's Mining Sector Demands Premium Engineering Plastics

Iron ore processing at Pilbara operations such as those in Port Hedland, Newman, and Tom Price involves moving millions of tonnes of abrasive ore through crushers, screens, conveyors, and chutes every year. Standard steel components wear out rapidly under these conditions, creating costly downtime and maintenance cycles. UHMWPE (Ultra-High Molecular Weight Polyethylene) wear liners have become the material of choice for chute liners, hopper liners, and conveyor transfer points because they offer 3-7 times the wear life of steel at a fraction of the weight. Our UHMWPE sheets, available in thicknesses from 6mm to 150mm and widths up to 2000mm, are specifically engineered for the abrasive conditions found in Pilbara iron ore operations.

Polyurethane Screen Panels for Gold and Lithium Operations

Gold mining operations in Kalgoorlie, Leonora, and the Eastern Goldfields rely heavily on vibrating screens for ore classification and dewatering. Polyurethane screen panels have largely replaced traditional woven wire screens in these applications due to their superior wear life, reduced blinding, and lower noise levels. Our polyurethane screen panels are cast from high-quality MDI-based polyurethane with Shore A hardness options from 40A to 90A, allowing precise tuning for different ore types and moisture conditions. For lithium spodumene operations in the Pilgangoora and Greenbushes regions, we supply custom-profiled polyurethane panels designed for the specific gravity and abrasivity of spodumene concentrate.

Ground Protection Mats for Remote Site Construction

Western Australia's vast distances and remote mine sites present unique challenges for temporary access road construction. Our UHMWPE ground protection mats (also known as rig mats or crane mats) provide stable working platforms for heavy equipment on soft, wet, or environmentally sensitive ground. Measuring 1200mm × 2400mm × 20mm as standard, with custom sizes available, these mats are used extensively at mine site expansions, pipeline construction projects, and infrastructure upgrades across the Pilbara, Kimberley, and Mid-West regions.
